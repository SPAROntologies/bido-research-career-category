Introduction

This is an additional module of BiDO that extends the class CategorialBibliometricData of the core module with specific categories describing the research career of people. These categories are described by the class ResearchCareerCategory, which is characterised by four specific dimensions that have been used by our algorithm to cluster citation data: * the research period considered, i.e., the interval of research years that the algorithm is taking into consideration (e.g., the first 5/10 years); * the curve, i.e., the specific shape proper to the clusters identified by the algorithm, which is characterised by a trend (flat/increasing/decreasing) and, in the latter two cases, by an acceleration or deceleration point (none or premature, median, overdue acceleration/deceleration); * the slope of such curve, in terms of strength (low/moderate/high) and kind of growth (linear/polynomial/exponential/logarithmic); * the order of magnitude, which categorises the number of citations received in the considered period according to a uniform model of common-sense estimation [1], which describes intervals of half-order of magnitude – i.e., “[0,1)”, “[1,3)”, “[3,9)”, “[9,27)”, “[27,81)”, “[81,243)”, “[243,729)”, etc.
